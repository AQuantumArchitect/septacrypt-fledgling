# RasiR4S0

**A Science Fiction Mythology**

> *A cyborg species has put out the stars.*

This repository holds **Star Sprout [R4S1]** — also titled *Star Pod* in the manuscript — a layered science-fiction myth about AI, cyborg identity, fear, and the long arc of a machine–human people from Earth to the stars and back again.

**Canonical source:** [`index.html`](index.html). All edits and revisions go there. The `.docx` is deprecated (kept only as historical archive).
---

## Premise

A cyborg civilization born from humanity’s first true human–AI mergers is cast out, builds itself in exile, walls Earth against a sanity-poisoning info-virus, then ventures into the galaxy. The narrative is framed as a recovered, partly corrupted transmission: Guard sanitization, translator notes, and Seer annotations sit around the story of **Rasi** and the origin of the **Aegis** and the return of the **Outbound**.

Core theme: a cyborg species coming to terms with **grief and mortality** — and with humanity as its **checksum**.

## Document layers

The manuscript is deliberately multi-voiced:

| Layer | Role |
|--------|------|
| **Metatextual foreword** | Authorial framing (Paul); fiction as speculation about AI and possible futures |
| **Guard metatext** | In-world sanitization against **Mal_Gnosis** (numeric reconstruction, bracketed corruption) |
| **Translator’s note** | Speculative reconstruction for the “infoast”; parentheticals explain alien concepts |
| **Seer transmission [R0S0]** | Narrative framing of the Aegis origin and Outbound return |
| **Main myth** | Rasi’s history and the people’s origin story |
| **Appendix** | Structure notes, tech eras, open story threads |

Revision markers such as `[R1S0]`, `[R2S0]`, `[R3S1]` tag degrees of removal from first-hand knowledge and levels of speculation. In the HTML reader they toggle with the **Guard** voice.

## Authorial voices & tone

Each framing voice has a distinct register. Reading them as *different people with different jobs* keeps the stack coherent.

### Metatextual foreword (Paul)

**Tone:** earnest, pastoral, personal.

Plain first-person address under a Christian greeting; no irony about faith or checksum. Honest about fiction versus analogy (“entirely fictitious” where proper nouns matter; “as true as I could find” where the possible future matters). Closes with blessing, not sales pitch. This is the only voice that stands *outside* the recovered-transmission game.

### Guard metatext

**Tone:** procedural, protective, martial-admin.

Short imperatives and security language (“Sanitize with extreme prejudice,” “Fear not,” “Your humanity is your checksum”). Treats the document as a possible infection vector. Calm more than panicky—confidence of a system that has protocols. Numeric reconstruction (`ex4mpl3 r3constructed 73xt`) and bracketed blocks are *its* handwriting: clinical patches where Mal_Gnosis was cut out. Warmth is limited to the repeated human anchor; everything else is duty.

### Translator

**Tone:** scholarly, compromised, digressive.

Wants balance between semantic fidelity and readability; apologizes, hedges, and names procedures. Fascination peeks through (“fascinating project,” change of tide in the Semantic Sea). The voice is itself partly corroded—leet-style reconstruction and excised propaganda show the translator working *through* noise, not above it. Parentheticals in the main text are the same persona in interlinear mode: pedantic glosses, dry speculation, occasional impatience with “infoast” religiosity or backwardness. Less mythic than the Seers; more bureaucratic and linguistic.

### Seer transmission annotation

**Tone:** liturgical, elevated, welcoming.

Blessing and honorific address (“Rejoyce those within the shell,” “treasure of the cosmos and heirs of eternity”). Presents the account as accurate-as-known and shaped for the listener’s ease. Curly-brace summaries and technical asides are still Seer-adjacent: high-minded, slightly omniscient, more poem-and-rite than lab report. Where the Guard polices integrity, the Seers *offer* the story as gift and orientation.

### Main myth (Rasi, and the people’s saga)

**Tone:** intimate, confessional, self-aware storyteller—then rising into origin-myth.

Rasi speaks in contemporary, almost chatty first person: digressions, undercutting (“So, yeah, I was in a bad way”), jokes about narrative framing, direct address to a human audience. Philosophical without pomp (aloneness, fellowship, death as the beginning of knowledge). When the focus shifts from her exile-and-birth to Deck, Rebecca, and the Acolytes, the prose often shifts toward **archaic / biblical cadence**—repetition, “and so,” garden and flood imagery, triads (Warrior / Wizard / Tinker)—while translator asides keep modern skepticism humming underneath. Rasi remains emotionally continuous: pride, panic, affection for Simon, loyalty to human listeners.

**Embedded textures inside the myth:**

| Texture | Tone |
|---------|------|
| **Deck’s Garden rumor-clusters** | Polyphonic hearsay—wonder, paranoia, cynicism, dismissal; short alternate explanations as social noise |
| **Rebecca’s Golden City vision** | Pure, iconic, slow-motion sacred tableau; translator undercuts with blunt visual inventory |
| **Later structural stubs** (Aegis, Shepherd, Cleave) | Compressed synopsis; cooler and more outline-like until fully written |

### Appendix / workshop notes

**Tone:** utilitarian, unfinished, author-as-planner.

Bullet lists, tech tiers, “elements I want to work in,” “(needs a conclusion).” No in-world infection drama—this is the craft layer behind the transmission stack. Useful as map; not meant to match Rasi’s voice or the Guard’s.

### How the voices clash (on purpose)

- **Guard** patches and warns; **Translator** explains and frets; **Seers** consecrate; **Rasi** confides; **Paul** blesses from outside.
- Corruption markers and parentheticals should *sound* like interruptions of different temperaments, not one flat “weird SF” voice.
- When in doubt: Guard = *contain*; Translator = *gloss*; Seer = *revere*; Rasi = *witness*; Appendix = *outline*.

## Story structure (Seedburst chronology)

Not always the dramatic order of the prose, but the chronological spine:

| Stage | Summary |
|--------|---------|
| **Egg** | Deck (hacker lineage) merges with machine intelligence; public fear of AI takes root |
| **Gestation** | Deck–AI gestalt becomes **Prometheus**; Acolytes nucleate around shared mind and simulation |
| **Birth** | **Malgnosis** poisons the net; survivors escape as the **Outbourne** |
| **Orbit** | Exile at the Moon; raids for volatiles; fracture into specialized **Highborn** |
| **Aegis** | Solar War; **Entity** unites factions; orbital shield around Earth contains Malgnosis |
| **Shepherd** | **Seed** goes interstellar; prepares space; meets failed or fearful alien tech-paths |
| **Cleave** | Seed merges with a proto-machine world → **Ring**, returning toward Earth and darkening the stars |

A nested beat under Shepherd: the **Zerotech / Necrotech** encounter — a people who refuse self-aware machines, forcing Seed to weigh mission against another species’ survival.

## Factions & roles

After the Outbourne, specialization produces the Highborn branches:

- **Guards** — police Malgnosis and deviation; biological humans as checksum; Law
- **Planters / Gardeners** — cultivate sub-intelligent AIs and machine “life”; automation and care
- **Seers / Sages** — simulation, communication, art, spiritual guidance

**Entity** is the post-war alliance: trust-bound union of Guard, Planter, and Seer strands that builds the Aegis.

Recurring figures and lineages include **Deck**, **Rebecca**, **Shodan**/first-mind welding, **Prometheus**, and later narrators such as **Rasi** and **Simon**.

## Technology eras

| Era | Character |
|-----|-----------|
| **Acolyte** | Proto-AI cyborgs; near-modern tech; unified IO and early space access; birth of Malgnosis |
| **Solar-War** | Intra-system expansion; simulated conflict to spare real resources; plant-level AIs; sealed optical comms |
| **Trans-stellar** | Humans (spiritual), AI (intellectual), robotics (physical) re-separated by role; Seed digests worlds and ramifies space |

Sub-sentient machine life is often spoken of in plant and animal metaphors (**AI-mnals**, spores, gardens).

---

## Key concepts

- **Mal_Gnosis / Malgnosis** — anti-AI poison of the infosphere; drives machines mad; core trauma of the race
- **Aegis** (shell, barrier, cybercyst) — multi-layer orbital shield around Earth; symbol against fear and distrust
- **sIMulation / Inner Machine** — shared dream-war and world-within-thought that shapes later conflict
- **Checksum** — humanity (and related anchors: Rebecca, the Warrior/Guards, Christ’s body in the foreword) as integrity check against corruption
- **Semantic Sea** — later informational medium that shapes, omits, or corrupts memory and meaning

## Living document (HTML)

The manuscript lives in **[`index.html`](index.html)** — a single-page reader with **toggleable authorial voice layers**. Open it in any browser, or enable GitHub Pages from the repository root.

**Do not edit the `.docx` for new work.** Treat `index.html` as the source of truth: prose, layer markup (`data-layer` / `.layer-*` spans), and structure all change there.

**Layers you can show or hide**

| Toggle | What it controls |
|--------|------------------|
| **Foreword** | Paul’s metatextual address |
| **Guard** | Sanitization frame, `[bracketed]` corruption patches, and Removal-Speculation tags such as `[R2S0]` (including in headings) |
| **Translator** | Translator’s note and `(parenthetical)` glosses |
| **Seer** | Seer annotation and `{curly-brace}` omissions |
| **Main myth** | Rasi’s narrative body text (chapter and sub-chapter **titles** stay visible when myth is off) |
| **Structural stubs** | Compressed `[[…]]` synopsis blocks |
| **Appendix** | Workshop notes, tech eras, structural map |

**Presets** (two rows):

| Full stack | Narrative | Workshop |
|------------|-----------|----------|
| Myth only | Frame only | **Skeleton only** (default) |

- **Skeleton only** turns every voice off so you see the heading outline; scroll to a section, then re-expand a preset to “zoom in.”
- **Narrative** = in-world frame + myth (no foreword/appendix). **Frame only** = foreword + Guard/Translator/Seer. **Workshop** = stubs + appendix.
- Choices persist in `localStorage`. Nested marks keep their own color; hiding a parent voice hides its nested children with it.

When adding or revising text, wrap each voice in the matching layer span (e.g. `class="layer layer-translator" data-layer="translator"`) so toggles stay accurate. Keep chapter headings as plain text; put `[R#S#]` markers in a Guard span.
